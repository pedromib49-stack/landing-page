# Conversion Landing Page Skill

This skill produces landing pages that are **both beautiful and functional** — pages that don't just look good but actively convert visitors into leads. The design serves the conversion goal; never the other way around.

Read this entire file before writing a single line of code.

---

## 0. Pre-Build Checklist

Before coding, answer these questions. If the user hasn't provided them, make reasonable assumptions and document them:

```
WHO is the target visitor? (first-time buyer? investor? renter?)
WHAT do they want? (find apartment? sell property? get consultation?)
WHAT do they fear? (being scammed? wasting time? overpaying?)
WHAT is the ONE action we want them to take? (WhatsApp? form? phone?)
WHAT proof do we have? (testimonials? years of experience? # of sales?)
WHAT is the scarcity or urgency? (limited inventory? market conditions?)
```

The answers shape every copy and design decision.

## 1. Conversion Architecture (Page Structure)

Build pages in this exact sequence. Each section has a job. Don't skip or reorder.

### Section 1 — Hero (Above the Fold)
**Job**: Make the visitor feel understood in 3 seconds. Capture attention, state the promise, trigger the first CTA.

Rules:
- Headline addresses the visitor's **desire or pain**, NOT the brand. Bad: "Ricardo Mendes, Corretor". Good: "Encontre o imóvel certo sem perder semanas em visitas que decepcionam."
- Subheadline delivers the mechanism: HOW you solve the pain.
- ONE primary CTA button — high contrast, action verb + benefit. Ex: "Falar com Ricardo no WhatsApp →"
- Social proof micro-element below the CTA: "★★★★★ 47 famílias atendidas em São Paulo"
- Hero image must be a REAL person (the agent), not a building or stock photo. The face = trust.
- No navigation menu (removes distractions). Logo only.

### Section 2 — Problem/Agitation
**Job**: Validate the visitor's frustration. Make them feel seen.

Rules:
- List 3-4 pains they recognize from experience (not generic complaints)
- Write in the visitor's language, not the agent's language
- End with a pivot: "Existe uma forma diferente de fazer isso."
- Keep it short: 80-120 words max

### Section 3 — Solution / Differentiator
**Job**: Present the agent/service as the specific answer to the specific pain.

Rules:
- 3 columns max, each = one concrete differentiator (not vague like "commitment" or "quality")
- Each differentiator needs a **proof point**: not "Rápido" but "Resposta em até 2h no WhatsApp"
- Include a secondary CTA here (same as primary)

### Section 4 — Process / How It Works
**Job**: Reduce friction by making the next step feel easy and predictable.

Rules:
- 3-4 numbered steps, written from the visitor's POV ("Você me conta…", "Eu filtro…", "Você visita apenas…")
- Keep steps concrete and action-oriented
- The last step should be the desired outcome, not the process (ex: "Você assina o contrato com segurança")
- Include a CTA after this section

### Section 5 — Social Proof
**Job**: Transfer trust from past clients to the current visitor.

Rules:
- Use REAL testimonials. If unavailable, create believable placeholders marked as [PLACEHOLDER - replace with real testimonials]
- Each testimonial needs: name, neighborhood/city, outcome (not just praise)
- Bad testimonial: "Ótimo profissional, super recomendo!" 
- Good testimonial: "Achei meu apartamento em 3 semanas depois de 4 meses procurando sozinha. Ricardo filtrou tudo e só me levou a ver 2 imóveis. Comprei o segundo." — Ana C., Pinheiros
- Numbers section: X anos de mercado | X negócios fechados | X% de clientes por indicação
- Logo/badges: CRECI, CBI, associations — legitimacy anchors

### Section 6 — About (Humanized)
**Job**: Build personal connection. People hire people, not services.

Rules:
- Real photo (different angle from hero)
- 3-4 sentences, first person, conversational tone
- Mention specific neighborhood/city knowledge
- Mention ONE personal detail that makes them relatable (time in the market, why they became an agent, a client story)
- CRECI number visible
- DO NOT use corporate language ("comprometido com excelência", "missão de superar expectativas")

### Section 7 — Featured Listings (optional)
**Job**: Provide concrete product examples, trigger imagination.

Rules:
- Only include if agent has real inventory to show
- 3-4 cards max: photo, price range, key stats (m², quartos, bairro)
- Each card has a CTA to contact about that specific property
- DO NOT make this the main focus of the page

### Section 8 — Final CTA
**Job**: Capture the visitors who scrolled all the way down but haven't converted yet.

Rules:
- Repeat the core promise from the hero (different wording)
- Include ONE form (name + phone + what they're looking for — 3 fields MAX) OR WhatsApp button
- Add urgency that is HONEST: "Geralmente respondo no mesmo dia", "Agenda limitada para essa semana"
- Social proof reminder: star rating or quick stat

### Section 9 — Footer
- Phone, WhatsApp, email, Instagram
- CRECI number
- Legal: minimal (privacy policy link only if form is present)
- NO complex navigation

## 2. Copywriting Principles

### Voice
- **Talk to one person**, not a crowd. Use "você", not "vocês".
- Write at a 8th-grade reading level. Short sentences. Active voice.
- Avoid superlatives with no proof: "o melhor", "o mais completo", "líder de mercado"
- Instead, use specifics: numbers, timeframes, neighborhoods, outcomes

### Headline Formula (pick the most fitting)
1. **Desire + Mechanism**: "Encontre [desejo] sem [dor]"
2. **Question**: "Cansado de [dor específica]?"
3. **Bold claim + qualifier**: "Você encontra seu imóvel em [X dias] — ou continuamos procurando"
4. **Social proof lead**: "47 famílias encontraram o lar ideal com Ricardo Mendes. A sua pode ser a próxima."

### CTA Copy Rules
- Never use generic verbs: "Enviar", "Clique aqui", "Saiba mais"
- Use action + benefit: "Quero falar com Ricardo →", "Ver imóveis disponíveis →", "Agendar visita gratuita →"
- WhatsApp CTAs perform best for Brazilian real estate. Use them.

### Trust Signals Hierarchy (use all that apply)
1. Real photo of the agent (highest trust)
2. CRECI number
3. Specific testimonials with outcomes
4. Number of transactions / years
5. Response time commitment ("Respondo em até 2h")
6. Neighborhood expertise markers

## 3. Design System for Conversion

### Visual Hierarchy Rules
Every element competes for attention. Establish strict hierarchy:
1. **Primary**: CTA buttons (must be the highest contrast element on screen)
2. **Secondary**: Headlines
3. **Tertiary**: Supporting copy and images
4. **Ghost**: Navigation, legal, footers

### Color Strategy
- Use a **dominant neutral** (off-white, warm white, or deep dark) as base
- ONE strong accent color for ALL CTAs — this trains the eye to recognize actions
- The accent must be the MOST saturated color on the page
- Accent suggestions for real estate trust tone: deep gold (#C9A84C), forest green (#2D5016), navy (#1B3A6B), terracotta (#C4622D)
- NEVER use more than 2 accent colors
- Body text must pass WCAG AA contrast

### Typography for Landing Pages
- Headline font: authoritative, distinct. Options: Playfair Display, DM Serif Display, Fraunces, Cormorant Garamond, Bebas Neue (bold/urban), Libre Baskerville
- Body font: clean and readable at 16-18px. Options: DM Sans, Lato, Source Sans Pro, Nunito
- NEVER use: Inter, Roboto, Arial, system-ui as primary fonts
- Line-height body: 1.6-1.8
- Max paragraph width: 65ch

### Spacing
- Generous section padding: 80-120px vertical on desktop, 48-64px on mobile
- Never crowd CTAs with adjacent elements: 32px minimum breathing room around buttons
- Use whitespace as a design element, not a bug

### Motion
- Page load: staggered fade-in for hero elements (delay: 0ms, 150ms, 300ms, 450ms)
- Scroll reveal: subtle translateY(20px) → translateY(0) with opacity fade for section entries
- CTA buttons: scale(1.02) on hover + box-shadow increase
- NO autoplay videos. NO parallax (kills mobile performance). NO heavy animations that delay CTA visibility.
- All animations must complete in < 600ms

### Mobile-First Non-Negotiables
- CTA button sticky at bottom on mobile (fixed position, disappears when form is visible)
- WhatsApp button: minimum 48px touch target
- Hero headline: maximum 2 lines on 375px width
- All images: lazy loaded, WebP when possible
- Font size: minimum 16px for body, 28px+ for H1 on mobile

## 4. Technical Standards

### Performance (Conversion Killer if Slow)
- No external JS frameworks for simple landing pages (vanilla JS is fine)
- Google Fonts: load max 2 font families, use `display=swap`
- Images: use `loading="lazy"` except hero image
- Inline critical CSS
- Target: < 2s First Contentful Paint

### WhatsApp Integration
Always pre-fill the WhatsApp message. Never make the user type from scratch.
```
https://wa.me/55XXXXXXXXXXX?text=Ol%C3%A1%2C+vim+pelo+site+e+gostaria+de+saber+mais+sobre+im%C3%B3veis
```

### Form Best Practices
- Maximum 3 fields for top-of-funnel: Name, Phone, Intention (dropdown)
- Phone field: auto-format as (XX) XXXXX-XXXX
- Submit button: full width on mobile
- Confirmation: inline success message (not redirect), trigger WhatsApp open after 1.5s delay
- Never ask for email as the primary contact in Brazilian real estate (WhatsApp is the channel)

### Accessibility
- All images: descriptive alt text
- Color contrast: WCAG AA minimum
- Focus states visible on all interactive elements
- Semantic HTML: h1→h2→h3 hierarchy respected

## 5. Anti-Patterns (Never Do These)

❌ **Generic hero headlines** — "Bem-vindo ao nosso site", "Encontre seu imóvel dos sonhos" (overused)
❌ **Logo as hero** — the agent's face converts better than a logo
❌ **Multiple CTAs competing in the same section** — pick ONE action per section
❌ **Social proof without specifics** — "Ótimo profissional" is worthless
❌ **Autoplay media** — kills mobile performance and annoys users
❌ **Full navigation menu** — gives visitors escape routes
❌ **Form with more than 3 fields** — every extra field reduces conversion ~10%
❌ **Vague differentiators** — "Compromisso", "Qualidade", "Experiência" without proof
❌ **Purple gradients on white** — the most overused AI aesthetic
❌ **Stock photos of buildings or families** — use real photos or high-quality illustrations
❌ **Chatbot popup on load** — destroys trust for personal brand pages
❌ **Fake urgency** — "Oferta expira em 00:23:47" countdown timers

## 6. Conversion Optimization Checklist

Run this before finalizing the page:

**Above the fold**
- [ ] Can a stranger understand what this person does in 3 seconds?
- [ ] Is the headline about the visitor's desire, not the agent's identity?
- [ ] Is the CTA visible without scrolling on mobile?
- [ ] Is there at least one trust signal above the fold?

**Copy**
- [ ] Does every section have ONE job?
- [ ] Are CTAs action verbs + benefit (not generic)?
- [ ] Are testimonials specific (outcome + timeframe + name)?
- [ ] Is the agent's voice human and specific (not corporate)?

**Design**
- [ ] Is there ONE clear accent color used only for CTAs?
- [ ] Is the CTA the highest-contrast element on each screen?
- [ ] Does the page work on 375px mobile width?
- [ ] Are font sizes readable on mobile (16px+ body)?

**Technical**
- [ ] WhatsApp link has pre-filled message?
- [ ] Images have alt text?
- [ ] Fonts load with display=swap?
- [ ] Form has maximum 3 fields?

## 7. Real Estate Specific Context (Brazil)

- WhatsApp is the #1 conversion channel — always the primary CTA
- CRECI number is a legal trust signal — always visible
- Brazilians respond to specificity about neighborhood expertise ("especialista em Pinheiros e Vila Madalena")
- Price range or "a partir de" performs better than "consulte valores" for buyer pages
- Seller pages should lead with: "Quanto vale seu imóvel? Avaliação gratuita"
- "Lançamentos" audiences respond to exclusivity: "lista VIP", "condições especiais para primeiros compradores"
- Trust is built through personal photo + CRECI + response time promise — in that order
